> There are all manner of lifestyles that are built around the pursuit of endless novelty, such as those centered on drug use, sadomasochism, extreme sports, endless travel…. And what happens, very very very often, is that the pursuit of new experiences becomes in and of itself the boring slog that all of the rest of us experience too. [...] _novelty itself ceases to be novel._ I don’t know if you’ve ever talked with people who have just spent like three or five years straight traveling, “walking the earth,” etc. For self-defensive purposes, they’ll usually assert how it was life-changing etc etc, and it probably was. But if you dig deeper, they will very often admit that they came to find it all enervating, a grind, that over time the novelty had a directly counterproductive impact to the point that it ended up dulling sensations rather than burnishing them. Meta-experience, as well as experience, is entirely capable of becoming just another grind.
